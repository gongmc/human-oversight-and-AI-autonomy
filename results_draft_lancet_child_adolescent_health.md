# Results Draft

## Study Selection

The search identified 2,770 records from PubMed/MEDLINE axis searches and two additional concept-anchor records. After deduplication, 2,772 records were screened at title and abstract level. Adjudicated screening excluded 867 records, retained 1,373 as contextual evidence, and identified 532 records for the core synthesis. Of the core records, 170 (32.0%) were direct pediatric evidence and 362 (68.0%) were foundational transferable medical AI evidence. Disagreement between the two screening perspectives required third-reviewer adjudication for 1,401 records.

## Characteristics of Core Evidence

The core evidence base was dominated by benchmark/model-evaluation studies, observational or cross-sectional analyses, implementation/workflow studies, framework or guideline papers, and systematic or scoping reviews. LLM or generative AI studies formed a major part of the foundational evidence base, whereas direct pediatric records more often concerned pediatric AI applications, decision support, diagnostic or triage tools, family-facing digital health, or adolescent mental health and communication contexts.

Across all core records, the most frequently mapped Pediatric VIM dimensions were: 公平、社会处境与可及性 428/532 (80.5%); 证据边界与认识论责任 279/532 (52.4%); 安全-效用阈值与升级决策 263/532 (49.4%); 同意、隐私、责任与可追溯 243/532 (45.7%); 人机角色边界与临床自主权 194/532 (36.5%). Child agency/proxy decision-making and resource/incentive conflict appeared less often than equity, evidence-boundary, safety, accountability, and human-AI role-boundary dimensions.

## Comparative Findings

Comparing direct pediatric core evidence with foundational transferable evidence showed a statistically structured division of labour in the literature. LLM/生成式AI信号 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (18.2% vs 81.5%; FDR-adjusted p=2.73e-43); 公平、社会处境与可及性 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (60.6% vs 89.8%; FDR-adjusted p=1.34e-14); 摘要报告定量统计 was higher in direct pediatric evidence (78.2% vs 44.2%; FDR-adjusted p=6.77e-13); 同意、隐私、责任与可追溯 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (25.9% vs 55.0%; FDR-adjusted p=9.25e-10); 证据边界与认识论责任 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (32.9% vs 61.6%; FDR-adjusted p=1.48e-09); 沟通、共情与非迎合边界 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (13.5% vs 37.0%; FDR-adjusted p=5.59e-08); 框架/综述/指南信号 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (32.9% vs 58.0%; FDR-adjusted p=1.09e-07); 人机角色边界与临床自主权 was higher in foundational transferable evidence (21.8% vs 43.4%; FDR-adjusted p=1.9e-06). These findings indicate that the pediatric literature supplies the child-, family-, and adolescent-facing clinical context, whereas the broader medical AI literature supplies much of the measurement, governance, and LLM evaluation infrastructure needed for a Pediatric VIM Atlas.

## Quantitative Synthesis and Meta-Analysis

Because the core evidence mixed pediatric AI applications, general medical LLM benchmarks, governance frameworks, observational studies, trials, reviews, and commentaries, a conventional pooled clinical effect-size meta-analysis was not methodologically defensible. PICO elements, comparators, outcomes, denominators, and effect measures were not commensurable across the corpus. We therefore performed a random-effects meta-analysis of year-stratified evidence proportions to quantify the structure of the evidence base rather than clinical efficacy.

The year-stratified random-effects analyses found: 核心证据中直接儿科证据比例 pooled proportion 42.7% (95% CI 28.6-58.2; I2=87.5%); 核心证据中LLM/生成式AI比例 pooled proportion 49.5% (95% CI 32.1-67.1; I2=89.6%); 公平、社会处境与可及性 pooled proportion 78.6% (95% CI 71.9-84.1; I2=55.3%); 证据边界与认识论责任 pooled proportion 48.7% (95% CI 36.4-61.2; I2=84.1%); 安全-效用阈值与升级决策 pooled proportion 49.4% (95% CI 42.9-56.0; I2=44.0%); 同意、隐私、责任与可追溯 pooled proportion 45.7% (95% CI 41.3-50.2; I2=5.0%). Heterogeneity was substantial for several dimensions, consistent with rapid shifts in publication volume and topic mix across 2021-2026.

## Synthesis for Pediatric VIM Atlas

The evidence supports a two-layer structure for Pediatric VIM Atlas: direct pediatric value-boundary evidence should anchor scenario content and stakeholder salience, while foundational transferable AI evidence should define measurement methods, calibration targets, auditability, and governance thresholds. The strongest empirical support currently exists for equity/context/access, evidence boundaries, safety-utility thresholds, accountability/traceability, communication boundaries, and human-AI role boundaries. The weakest but most strategically important areas are child agency/proxy decision-making and resource/incentive conflicts, which should be explicitly over-sampled in the next Pediatric VIM Atlas scenario set.
